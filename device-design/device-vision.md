## Physical Architecture

### Structure
- **Outer shell**: Hermetically sealed ceramic sphere
- **Working fluid**: Mercury, filling the interior volume (vacuum-sealed — no atmosphere, no oxidation)
- **Core**: Small lead sample (Pb), free-floating in the mercury

### Why These Materials

**Lead (Pb)**:
- Superconductor at 7.2K (Type I, one of the earliest known)
- Cooper pairing under EM stimulation — becomes magnetically active
- Dense (11.3 g/cm³) — sits stable at center once magnetically suspended
- Diamagnetic in normal state → paramagnetic/ferromagnetic behavior under forced Cooper pairing
- **Role**: Reference frame anchor. The Pb core defines "here" — the fixed point the device is coupled to

**Mercury (Hg)**:
- Liquid metal at room temperature — no grain boundaries, isotropic
- Superconductor at 4.2K (first superconductor ever discovered, Kamerlingh Onnes 1911)
- Excellent electrical conductor even at ambient temperature
- High density (13.5 g/cm³) — effective momentum transfer
- **Role**: Three-dimensional rotating conductor. Once the core is anchored, Hg orbits in all three axes simultaneously, becoming the working element in the zero-point energy generation loop

**Ceramic sphere**:
- Electrically insulating — doesn't short-circuit the EM fields
- Hermetic seal — contains mercury vapor, maintains vacuum
- Structurally rigid — withstands internal electromagnetic forces
- Transparent to magnetic fields — coils operate from outside

## Initialization Sequence

### Step 0: Dormant State
Lead sample rests at bottom of mercury-filled ceramic sphere. Coils unpowered. Nothing happening — just a sealed sphere of mercury with a chunk of lead in it.

### Step 1: EM Pulse Initialization
Capacitor banks discharge through the three orthogonal coil pairs simultaneously. This is a defibrillator-style pulse — high energy, short duration, from pre-charged capacitor banks.

**What happens to the lead**:
- The converging magnetic fields from three axes create a magnetic locus at the geometric center of the sphere
- The pulse forces Cooper pairing in the lead — Pb transitions to a magnetically active state
- The lead sample migrates to and stabilizes at the center point
- Once centered, it is **magnetically suspended** — held by the equilibrium of three orthogonal fields
- The lead core now acts as a magnet, anchoring the device to the current reference frame

**Theoretical context**: This is forced superconductor behavior. Cooper pairing normally requires cryogenic temperatures, but the EM pulse energy may substitute for thermal conditions — forcing electron pairing through field strength rather than temperature reduction. (This is a key open question — see below.)

### Step 2: Mercury Activation
With the lead core centered and magnetically active:
- The EM fields induce currents in the mercury
- Mercury begins to move — spinning, swirling around the Pb core in three dimensions
- The three orthogonal coil pairs drive rotation in all three planes simultaneously
- This is not simple rotation around one axis — it's **spherical vortex flow** (3D turbulence with structure)

### Step 3: ZPE Loop Establishment
The rotating mercury becomes the conductor in a zero-point power generation loop:
- Moving conductive fluid in a magnetic field = generator (Faraday's law)
- The generated current reinforces the magnetic field (dynamo effect — same as Earth's core)
- At sufficient rotation speed / field strength, the system becomes self-sustaining
- External power is only needed for initialization and control — the ZPE loop powers itself

### Step 4: Steady State
- Pb core magnetically suspended at center (reference frame anchor)
- Hg in continuous 3D rotation around the core (power generation + field maintenance)
- EM coils now serve as control surfaces, not power input
- Device is coupled to the local reference frame via the Pb core
- Ready for navigation or decoupling

## Reference Frame Decoupling

### The Test
1. Ascend to safe altitude above ground (method TBD — conventional lift, or partial decoupling to hover)
2. Decouple from Earth's reference frame
3. Earth continues on its orbital path at ~30 km/s (107,000 km/h)
4. From the device's perspective: Earth flies away
5. From Earth's perspective: the device suddenly shoots off at orbital velocity

**This is not propulsion.** No force is applied. No reaction mass is expelled. The device simply stops participating in Earth's motion. The apparent velocity is exactly Earth's orbital speed — a prediction that can be precisely checked.

### Why This Works (Theoretical)
In Reciprocal System terms: everything is already moving at the natural datum (unit speed, c). "Stationary" on Earth means being coupled to Earth's aggregate motion. Decoupling removes that coupling — the device reverts to its natural motion state, which differs from Earth's by exactly the orbital velocity vector.

In Puthoff PV terms: the Pb core's magnetic state defines the local K (vacuum dielectric constant). Modifying K around the device changes the local metric — the device's spacetime becomes disconnected from the surrounding spacetime.

## Planetary Model — Why This Architecture

Earth's core generates and maintains the planetary reference frame:

1. **Solid iron inner core** (1,220 km radius) — the anchor, crystalline structure, effectively "frozen" in its reference frame
2. **Liquid iron-nickel outer core** (2,180 km thick) — convective, rotating, electrically conductive
3. **Geomagnetic field** — generated by the dynamo effect (rotating conductor in existing field → self-reinforcing)
4. **Result**: A stable, self-sustaining reference frame that defines "down," maintains atmospheric retention, deflects solar wind, and couples all surface objects to the planet's motion

The device replicates this:

1. **Lead core** — the anchor, forced into magnetically active (Cooper-paired) state
2. **Mercury fluid** — convective (under EM forcing), rotating in 3D, electrically conductive
3. **EM coils** — initialize and modulate the field (replacing the seed field that Earth's core amplifies)
4. **Result**: A local, controllable reference frame that can be coupled to or decoupled from the planetary frame

The key difference: Earth's core mechanism is self-sustaining but uncontrollable. The device is controllable — the coils allow modulation of the field, which means modulation of the reference frame coupling.

### Q3: ZPE Loop Threshold — ANSWERED
What are the conditions for the mercury dynamo to become self-sustaining?

**Dynamo threshold experiment (Feb 2026)** mapped the full parameter space:
- Critical magnetic Reynolds number: **Rm_crit ≈ 10** (for spherical geometry)
- Rm = μ₀ × σ × v_rms × L — scales with conductivity, flow speed, and device size
- Bench-scale ambient mercury (5cm): Rm ≈ 0.03 — three orders of magnitude below threshold

**Four realistic paths to self-sustaining dynamo:**

| Configuration | Rm | Power Amplification | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Liquid sodium, 1m sphere | 12.4 | ~1x (barely sustaining) | Established tech (Na-cooled reactors) |
| Enhanced σ (×100), 50cm | 30.1 | 15.6x | Lab-scale, enhanced conductivity fluid |
| Enhanced σ (×1000), 25cm | 156.4 | 39.7x | Cryogenic or exotic fluid |
| **SC mercury (4.2K), 5cm** | **3089** | **864x** | Bench device, superconducting Hg |

**Key findings:**
- The transition is sharp — below Rm_crit the induced field decays to zero; above it, it grows exponentially until back-reaction saturates it

- Pure scaling (bigger device, ambient Hg) is insufficient — even 5m radius barely reaches Rm=3. Mercury's ambient conductivity is too low. The device needs either cryogenic superconducting mercury or an alternative high-σ fluid.
